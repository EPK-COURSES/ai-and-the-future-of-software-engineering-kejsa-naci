## 1. Introduction

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is rapidly becoming one of the most important technologies influencing the domain of software engineering. The emergence of large language models and AI-driven development tools has revolutionized how software is conceived, created, verified, and operated. Large language models and AI-assisted development tools, such as GitHub Copilot and ChatGPT, can augment the ability of software engineers by generating code, proposing solutions, drafting documentation, and aiding debugging (Peng et al., 2023). Consequently, the daily routines of software development teams are increasingly infused with AI.

The increasing use of AI is generating a great deal of debate and conversation concerning the role of software engineers in the future. While some contend that AI may displace software engineers entirely, a growing body of literature supports the idea that AI will fundamentally change the nature of software engineering rather than completely replace it (Li et al., 2024). Though AI tools may automate some rote coding tasks, they still necessitate human oversight, judgment, and problem-solving capabilities. Software engineering extends far beyond simply writing code to include system design, communication, decision-making, and understanding user requirements.

The effect of AI is particularly profound for software engineers in the future. Due to the ability of AI tools to complete many basic coding tasks that previously allowed junior developers to develop their professional skills, junior developers are now encountering new obstacles. On the other hand, experienced engineers have become more valuable than ever because businesses require people to assess and improve the outputs generated by AI tools and manage the complexity of software systems (Hou et al., 2024). In addition, the rapid evolution of AI technologies is producing novel job roles and specialized engineering positions.

In this paper, the impact of Artificial Intelligence on the jobs and career prospects in software engineering will be investigated. Specifically, we will explore the shifting roles of junior engineers, the changing expectations of experienced engineers, new AI-centric career fields, the automation of repetitive tasks, and the importance of human skills in an AI-driven environment. 

## 2. The Impact of AI on Software Engineering Jobs

Artificial intelligence has found an indispensable place in software engineering today and its impact is enormous on the ways we build software. It aids software engineers in coding, bug detection, testing, writing documentation, and in other software development activities. The assistance from these AI tools in software development leads to reduced completion time of many tasks, thereby increasing efficiency. Numerous studies has revealed that programming tasks using AI-supported coding tools can be finished much faster than when using other methods (Peng et al., 2023).

The increase in utilization of AI technologies has resulted in the automation of repetitive, laborious and time-consuming tasks. Tasks such as the generation of code, writing unit tests, generation of technical documentation, etc. Can be automated to some extent through the usage of large language models and AI development tools (Sobania et al., 2022). This leaves the software engineer with more time to focus on higher-level concerns such as software architecture, system design and resolving complicated technical problems. In the long run, the role of software engineers will more shift from tasks of manual implementation to ones of supervision and strategic design.

The rise in AI has also caused a change in expectations for software engineers. Today employers expect software engineers to effectively interact with AI-based development tools, while retaining the underlying knowledge of software engineering practices. It is the duty of the software engineer to review and assess the results provided by an AI tool to verify their correctness and find any potential vulnerabilities or quality issues (Hou et al., 2024). In such circumstances, a good logical and technical sense of reasoning is still indispensable despite advancement in automation.

Despite all the good brought upon by AI, there are fears that certain roles in the software engineering industry might cease to exist. The tasks that would have otherwise been completed by a junior developer, can be completed much faster using an AI assistant. However current researches suggests that AI may instead bring about a modification of software engineering jobs rather than an entire displacement of software engineers (Li et al., 2024) since it requires human skill and communication to be in charge of decision-making, creativity and ethics, and business logic that the AI tool cannot provide.

## 3. Changing Roles of Junior Software Developers

Artificial intelligence is fundamentally changing the role of junior software developers within the software engineering industry. Historically, entry-level developers learned through carrying out tasks such as bug fixing, writing small tests, implementing simple logic, and maintenance of software code. However many of these duties can now be more efficiently completed using development tools powered by AI, for instance the use of large language models to automatically write code, clarify programming concepts, and identify software issues decreases the scope of manual tasks for less experienced individuals (Vaithilingam et al., 2022).

As software development becomes increasingly integrated with AI, employers now expect junior developers to contribute more than just writing lines of code. Junior developers now need to consider the architectural design, problem-solving methodologies and the use of best practices in software development rather than merely focusing on implementation; the ability to think analytically and critically assess the suggestions made by an AI will be essential for newcomers (Hou et al., 2024).

Another issue that arises is the dependency of the junior developer on AI tools throughout the learning process. Although an AI can improve learning rates and enhance efficiency, excessive reliance on code generated by AI may weaken understanding of fundamental programming concepts. Evidence suggests that while AI tools may act as useful support aids in novice programming, they cannot replace the detailed knowledge required to resolve problems in the field of software engineering (Kazemitabaar et al., 2023); therefore junior developers need to be equipped with the ability to solve problems in terms of programming, debugging and analysis and not rely on the assistance of AI.

These challenges come with new opportunities as AI can also be used as learning assistants for junior software developers; this technology can help beginners learn a new technology or explore new approaches to solving problems, thus allowing the developer to become productive quicker. The developer who becomes proficient in working alongside an AI system may attain an advantage within the future job market, therefore the role of junior software developers will not vanish, but shift toward a more combined approach utilizing technical ability, flexibility and competence with the use of artificial intelligence.

In conclusion, the AI is currently transforming what it means to be a junior software developer; a foundation of technical skill alongside a proficient command of working with AI will best prepare new entrants for a career within the software engineering profession.
