## 1. Introduction

Artificial Intelligence is rapidly becoming one of the most important technologies influencing the domain of software engineering. The emergence of large language models and AI-driven development tools has revolutionized how software is conceived, created, verified, and operated. Large language models and AI-assisted development tools, such as GitHub Copilot and ChatGPT, can augment the ability of software engineers by generating code, proposing solutions, drafting documentation, and aiding debugging (Peng et al., 2023). Consequently, the daily routines of software development teams are increasingly infused with AI.

The increasing use of AI is generating a great deal of debate and conversation concerning the role of software engineers in the future. While some contend that AI may displace software engineers entirely, a growing body of literature supports the idea that AI will fundamentally change the nature of software engineering rather than completely replace it (Li et al., 2024). Though AI tools may automate some rote coding tasks, they still necessitate human oversight, judgment, and problem-solving capabilities. Software engineering extends far beyond simply writing code to include system design, communication, decision-making, and understanding user requirements.

The effect of AI is particularly profound for software engineers in the future. Due to the ability of AI tools to complete many basic coding tasks that previously allowed junior developers to develop their professional skills, junior developers are now encountering new obstacles. On the other hand, experienced engineers have become more valuable than ever because businesses require people to assess and improve the outputs generated by AI tools and manage the complexity of software systems (Hou et al., 2024). In addition, the rapid evolution of AI technologies is producing novel job roles and specialized engineering positions.

In this paper, the impact of Artificial Intelligence on the jobs and career prospects in software engineering will be investigated. Specifically, we will explore the shifting roles of junior engineers, the changing expectations of experienced engineers, new AI-centric career fields, the automation of repetitive tasks, and the importance of human skills in an AI-driven environment. 

## 2. The Impact of AI on Software Engineering Jobs

AI has found an indispensable place in software engineering today and its impact is enormous on the ways we build software. It aids software engineers in coding, bug detection, testing, writing documentation, and in other software development activities. The assistance from these AI tools in software development leads to reduced completion time of many tasks, thereby increasing efficiency. Numerous studies has revealed that programming tasks using AI-supported coding tools can be finished much faster than when using other methods (Peng et al., 2023).

The increase in utilization of AI technologies has resulted in the automation of repetitive, laborious and time-consuming tasks. Tasks such as the generation of code, writing unit tests, generation of technical documentation, etc. Can be automated to some extent through the usage of large language models and AI development tools (Sobania et al., 2022). This leaves the software engineer with more time to focus on higher-level concerns such as software architecture, system design and resolving complicated technical problems. In the long run, the role of software engineers will more shift from tasks of manual implementation to ones of supervision and strategic design.

The rise in AI has also caused a change in expectations for software engineers. Today employers expect software engineers to effectively interact with AI-based development tools, while retaining the underlying knowledge of software engineering practices. It is the duty of the software engineer to review and assess the results provided by an AI tool to verify their correctness and find any potential vulnerabilities or quality issues (Hou et al., 2024). In such circumstances, a good logical and technical sense of reasoning is still indispensable despite advancement in automation.

Despite all the good brought upon by AI, there are fears that certain roles in the software engineering industry might cease to exist. The tasks that would have otherwise been completed by a junior developer, can be completed much faster using an AI assistant. However current researches suggests that AI may instead bring about a modification of software engineering jobs rather than an entire displacement of software engineers (Li et al., 2024) since it requires human skill and communication to be in charge of decision-making, creativity and ethics, and business logic that the AI tool cannot provide.

## 3. Changing Roles of Junior Software Developers

Artificial intelligence is fundamentally changing the role of junior software developers within the software engineering industry. Historically, entry-level developers learned through carrying out tasks such as bug fixing, writing small tests, implementing simple logic, and maintenance of software code. However many of these duties can now be more efficiently completed using development tools powered by AI, for instance the use of large language models to automatically write code, clarify programming concepts, and identify software issues decreases the scope of manual tasks for less experienced individuals (Vaithilingam et al., 2022).

As software development becomes increasingly integrated with AI, employers now expect junior developers to contribute more than just writing lines of code. Junior developers now need to consider the architectural design, problem-solving methodologies and the use of best practices in software development rather than merely focusing on implementation; the ability to think analytically and critically assess the suggestions made by an AI will be essential for newcomers (Hou et al., 2024).

Another issue that arises is the dependency of the junior developer on AI tools throughout the learning process. Although an AI can improve learning rates and enhance efficiency, excessive reliance on code generated by AI may weaken understanding of fundamental programming concepts. Evidence suggests that while AI tools may act as useful support aids in novice programming, they cannot replace the detailed knowledge required to resolve problems in the field of software engineering (Kazemitabaar et al., 2023); therefore junior developers need to be equipped with the ability to solve problems in terms of programming, debugging and analysis and not rely on the assistance of AI.

These challenges come with new opportunities as AI can also be used as learning assistants for junior software developers; this technology can help beginners learn a new technology or explore new approaches to solving problems, thus allowing the developer to become productive quicker. The developer who becomes proficient in working alongside an AI system may attain an advantage within the future job market, therefore the role of junior software developers will not vanish, but shift toward a more combined approach utilizing technical ability, flexibility and competence with the use of artificial intelligence.

In conclusion, the AI is currently transforming what it means to be a junior software developer; a foundation of technical skill alongside a proficient command of working with AI will best prepare new entrants for a career within the software engineering profession.

## 4. Future Expectations from Senior Engineers

In an environment where Artificial Intelligence is ever more prevalent, Senior Engineers become indispensable part of development teams. Even if most standard coding work is outsourced to AI tools, crucial technical choices and quality assurance still rest on the shoulder of human Senior Engineers. While organizations will start embracing AI powered development environments, Senior Engineers are expected to lead the teams in employing these technologies effectively, keeping performance, security and reliability of the systems intact (Peng et al., 2023).

One of the crucial jobs the Senior Engineer will do is to audit AI generated code. The rapid code generation can result in incorrect or inefficient, sometimes unsafe, outputs which are to be verified by experienced humans. So, the expert engineer must verify the correctness of the AI response to meet technical and organization's objectives (Sobania et al., 2022). This relies on a deep technical knowledge and software engineering concepts.

Beyond technical assessment of the system, another responsibility of Senior Engineers will evolve around system design and architecture. While AI tools can aid in writing code, it cannot assist in large-scale system design and architectural decisions which still require human cognition. An engineer needs to keep the scalabilty, maintainability, security and business requirements into consideration when making design decisions for the software (Hou et al., 2024).

Leadership and communication skills too will be a prominent requirement for Senior Engineers. Mentoring junior developers and managing team coordination will be more important, especially when teams adapt to new tech. They will also have to bridge the gap between new tech efficiency and human accountability.

To conclude, the role of Senior Engineers is not diminished due to the AI. Rather, their responsibilities will get enhanced to include crucial technical decision making and system architecture which require experience and deep understanding of software engineering to effectively complement the capabilities brought by AI.

## 5. Emerging AI-Related Engineering Roles

Artificial Intelligence is opening up new jobs within the software engineering industry. As AI is being more heavily implemented within businesses, the need for individuals that know how to develop software alongside AI will rise, creating specialized engineering job roles that previously did not exist or were not as common (Nascimento et al., 2019). One of the roles with the most growth is that of an AI Software Engineer who focuses on the development of software products containing AI models, and a Machine Learning Engineer who is responsible for designing, developing, and maintaining machine learning models that will be part of software products (Li et al., 2024). With increased development with AI tools, there is also an increased demand for AI-assisted development specialists whose job role is to verify AI produced code, enhance development processes, and confirm AI is creating reliable and safe results (Hou et al., 2024). With increasing concern of AI, the roles of AI security engineers and AI ethics engineers are also being implemented to discuss issues of privacy, bias, transparency, and ethics in AI. As Artificial Intelligence development continues to advance, the skills needed to perform software engineering are also changing and engineers who understand how to develop both software and use AI will most likely have opportunities for a while.

 

## 6. Automation of Repetitive Software Engineering Tasks

One of the most prevalent impact of AI on the field of software engineering is its ability to automate redundant work in software development. With advancements in AI, it can help developers by generating code, documenting the software, suggesting corrections, writing test cases and providing support in debugging (Peng et al., 2023). All these are done relatively faster than a manual approach for software teams.

The Automation of tasks in the development process has sped up several software development practices. Instead of writing code manually over and over again, developers get to focus on more demanding activities like system design, system architecture and other problems encountered during software development (Vaithilingam et al., 2022). Studies have shown how AI code generation has decreased the amount of effort and time spent on writing code while improving the workflow and efficiency.

However, the suggestions given by the AI aren't always perfect and they often require human intervention. While AI systems are known to create code with bugs and vulnerabilities or produce outputs which do not adhere to the constraints or project requirements (Sobania et al., 2022), they also provide many opportunities for engineers to overcome problems in software development in an easier way, thus saving time and effort. Hence, human evaluation and testing is still an integral part of development process.

There are certain risks involved in an organization relying solely on automation. The reliance on automation tools could result in a lack of technical experience for the software engineers and their problem solving skills might weaken over a period of time (Denny et al., 2023). It is important to maintain a fine line between relying on automation and using one's own judgment while developing software.

## 7. Human Skills That AI Cannot Easily Replace

While AI is getting better at aiding software engineers, some skills are harder for it to learn. AI can create code, offer advice, and automate routine tasks, but it is far from replicating the skills needed for successful software engineering. Therefore some skills become more important than others with the rise of AI. The importance of a specific set of skills will continue to grow in the age of AI (Li et al., 2024).

The first most important human skill to possess is critical thinking. Software engineers will need to analyze output of an AI system, spot issues, and make critical decisions regarding solutions in a technical context. An AI might make a suggestion, but the decision of whether the suggestion is right and appropriate for the specific situation and requirement of the business lies solely on the engineer (Hou et al., 2024).

Communication skills are also a highly important skill AI will be slow to overcome. Software development is always a collaborative effort; engineers have to collaborate with other team members, managers, or even clients to work and deliver successfully. Comprehending requirements from users and expressing them clearly, making use of human-understanding for communication and working with emotional intelligence, are traits that modern AI does not possess (Storey et al., 2017).

Creativity is another indispensable trait of a human software engineer. AI can only create according to existing patterns and trends; it lacks human ability in innovation, designing new solutions, and overcoming novel problems through different approaches. Human leadership and ethics are also aspects where AI is not efficient and effective. Decisions relating to social impact, legality, and morality are of crucial importance and are expected from humans.

Finally, software engineering will see a synergy between human and AI, not a fight. Engineers with strong communication, critical thinking, creativity and leadership skills are, and will continue to be, very essential, despite advances in AI.

## 8. Personal Reflection and Future Career Adaptation

Upon reviewing literature regarding the implications of AI on software engineering jobs, it has become clear that Artificial Intelligence is more likely to reshape, not replace, software engineering roles. Before undertaking this study, I had predicted that eventually, AI would eventually usurp a majority of software engineering jobs, however, through the reviewed literature in this study, AI will more accurately automate many mundane, repetitive tasks, enhancing the productivity of the engineer, while human expertise will still be relied upon for creativity, critical thinking, communication and decision making.

Another surprising element from my findings was how integrated AI tools have become in the current software engineering profession, with engineers now using AI to generate code, help with debuggers and automate simple tasks. Whilst this is very useful and efficient, this can pose problems for entry level positions in engineering roles, as it has to be made sure that engineers build a strong foundational knowledge instead of solely relying on AI-generated programs (Denny et al. 2023).

As far as my future career is concerned, I can tell that my attitude towards it will now change slightly because I can clearly see the influence of AI, and know I will need to keep in touch with these changes if I am to succeed. I do not perceive AI as an immediate threat; rather I will use it as a means to increase productivity where it is beneficial to use. To succeed as a future engineer, I intend to enhance my current knowledge of programming, further my ability to problem solve, and to further develop skills concerning the software architecture, communications and teamwork elements of software engineering.

## 9. Conclusion

The use of Artificial Intelligence will be transformative to the software engineering profession, redefining how software is developed, and how engineers approach their day to day activities. AI-powered software engineering tools will allow for increased productivity, through the automation of time-consuming and tedious activities throughout the software development life cycle. However, through present studies it appears likely that rather than completely replace software engineers, AI will be more instrumental in the evolution of software engineering roles (Li et al., 2024).

This study highlights that across junior developers, senior engineers and teams the roles of those working in software engineering is likely to evolve. Through automation and delegation to AI-powered tools the execution of repetitive tasks will no longer define the majority of the software engineer’s job. Instead the skill sets necessary such as architecture, logical thought, communication, and leadership along with decision making are still a vital human attribute. The emergence of new technology is also creating a whole host of new engineering positions and careers for professionals who are willing to change and adapt along with the new technology.

For the software engineer, technical skills and human centered competencies will continue to shape future roles within the industry. A engineer's ability to collaborate with an AI whilst retaining key problem solving skills will likely set them up for success in the future of the software engineering field. Ultimately, the future is likely to be an integration of human engineer and the AI.
