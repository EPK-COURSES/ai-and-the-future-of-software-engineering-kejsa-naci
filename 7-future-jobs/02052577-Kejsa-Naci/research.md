## 1. Introduction

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is rapidly becoming one of the most important technologies influencing the domain of software engineering. The emergence of large language models and AI-driven development tools has revolutionized how software is conceived, created, verified, and operated. Large language models and AI-assisted development tools, such as GitHub Copilot and ChatGPT, can augment the ability of software engineers by generating code, proposing solutions, drafting documentation, and aiding debugging (Peng et al., 2023). Consequently, the daily routines of software development teams are increasingly infused with AI.

The increasing use of AI is generating a great deal of debate and conversation concerning the role of software engineers in the future. While some contend that AI may displace software engineers entirely, a growing body of literature supports the idea that AI will fundamentally change the nature of software engineering rather than completely replace it (Li et al., 2024). Though AI tools may automate some rote coding tasks, they still necessitate human oversight, judgment, and problem-solving capabilities. Software engineering extends far beyond simply writing code to include system design, communication, decision-making, and understanding user requirements.

The effect of AI is particularly profound for software engineers in the future. Due to the ability of AI tools to complete many basic coding tasks that previously allowed junior developers to develop their professional skills, junior developers are now encountering new obstacles. On the other hand, experienced engineers have become more valuable than ever because businesses require people to assess and improve the outputs generated by AI tools and manage the complexity of software systems (Hou et al., 2024). In addition, the rapid evolution of AI technologies is producing novel job roles and specialized engineering positions.

In this paper, the impact of Artificial Intelligence on the jobs and career prospects in software engineering will be investigated. Specifically, we will explore the shifting roles of junior engineers, the changing expectations of experienced engineers, new AI-centric career fields, the automation of repetitive tasks, and the importance of human skills in an AI-driven environment. 
## 2. The Impact of AI on Software Engineering Jobs

In the last few years artificial intelligence has brought major changes in the software engineering. Tools such as modern AI-powered technologies that aid developers to code, test applications, generate documentation or even debug applications are becoming widely available. As a consequence, software companies are now developing products in less time and are increasing the productivity of development teams [1]. AI tools that act as a coding assistant like GitHub Copilot or ChatGPT are now common tools for developers of all experience levels.

One of the most visible impacts that AI brought to software engineering is the automation of redundant tasks. Tasks such as auto-completing lines of code, performing basic debugging or writing simple documentation and test cases can now be partially automated by AI systems [6]. By reducing the amount of work devoted to such repetitive tasks, developers have more time to focus on more difficult problems, software design and decision making, instead of spending their time on programming. More and more companies are integrating AI to help developers automate tasks.

The adoption of AI is creating new expectations for the software engineering field. Developers are expected to know how to use the tools that AI provides instead of just focusing on traditional programming techniques [3]. Software engineers need to review the code written by the AI, ensure the quality and security of the software products and be able to verify and correct the produced code. The problem solving and critical thinking skills that the engineers have are becoming of paramount importance in the current world.

Even though AI improves development, the implementation of new AI tools also raise some questions about some software engineering jobs, especially entry level jobs [8]. It is perceived that some tasks previously performed by junior engineers can now be completed by AI. However many experts in this domain believe that AI will mostly transform software engineering positions rather than replace them [4]. The human component remains indispensable, particularly in terms of communication, design of the overall system and its leadership, creativity or understanding the needs of a business.
## 3. Changing Roles of Junior Software Developers
Artificial Intelligence has greatly impacted the software development junior role in the current technological domain. Previously, most of the basic development jobs (simple coding, error fixing, application testing and coding monotonous programs) were undertaken by junior developers. Now a few of these works can be done partly by using AI powered tools like GitHub Copilot and ChatGPT which gives out code predictions, explains programming concepts, and can write full code snippets in mere seconds [3]. As such, the expectations of junior developers are beginning to change.

An example of concern is that the reduction of entry level jobs available to new developers. This is due to the capability of AI to carry out many basic development tasks more quickly and with reduced staffing [8]. The consequence of this means there may be increased competition when attempting to secure entry level jobs as new developers may face increased competition for these roles. Some organizations are also beginning to expect junior developers to reach peak productivity faster because they have the support of an AI tool, and so require a better understanding of programming concepts, the ability to solve problems, and of debugging, than was previously necessary [4].

Another issue involves an over-reliance on these tools during learning. Many students or beginner developers are using an AI to write code instead of fully understanding how to write code itself. This has issues as programmers are still required to have fundamentals of coding and logic [4], and if they cannot write and produce code they will have issues with producing what's needed and solving complex software problems.

AI also creates opportunity in the junior developer role. A number of the AI driven tools can aid in faster learning, increase productivity, enhance understanding of programming concepts if used in the correct manner, or the developer can use the AI in more of a learning and experimental capacity as a virtual tutor instead of relying on them. People who have the ability to integrate with these systems will only gain more value in a software job market in the near future [10].

Ultimately the role of junior developers is changing, not diminishing. AI continues to take on the repetitive and mundane tasks within development, however human interaction and capabilities such as creativity, communication, critical thinking and problem solving of complex software are still required and these are jobs which AI will be incapable of performing in the foreseeable future. Junior developers willing to keep on learning will also continue to remain important figures in software development.

## 4. Future Expectations from Senior Engineers
AI has become the driving factor that raises the status of senior software engineers. The nature of AI can help automate the mundane and repetitive coding tasks, yet require senior engineers to monitor AI-generated codes, make tech decisions, and orchestrate large software architecture [1]. Businesses now depend on senior engineers to make sure AI-generated codes are safe, stable and correct in their purposes.

A critical responsibility for senior engineers now is verifying and examining the codes written by AI. While AI tools could enhance development speed and efficiency, they may also produce errors and insecure codes [4]. It is senior engineers who should review and confirm codes written by AI with professional knowledge and critical thinking ability to achieve higher quality of software system.

More importantly, software architect ability and leadership is becoming the increasingly crucial traits of senior engineers in AI era. They are supposed to manage and lead the entire dev team, educate junior members and architect highly scalable system which is not currently possible with AI [5]. Human are responsible for the communication, decision making and problem-solving part, which cannot be covered by AI.

Therefore, AI is definitely not leading to the decline of the importance of senior engineers but instead transforming the position and elevating the role in the development team.
